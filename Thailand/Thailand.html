North of Thailand

'Chiang Mai'

Thailand's second city - Smaller, more relaxed answer to Bangkok. The city is surrounded by natural perfection where suitable for travelers who seek a trekking adventure and a spiritual awakening of northern arts & cultures.



Doi Inthanon

The highest peak in Thailand, and the national park on mountain that surrounds several waterfalls and a hill tribe village are other attractions, along with two pagodas. Travelers can do some trekking and hike the mountain, or take a more leisurely route around the park.

Doi AngKhang

A natural tourist attraction, tourists can experience winter flowers, Phaya Sua Krong flowers espeacially Wild Himalayan Cherry or Sakura of Thailand and many more, sea of fog, strawberry farm, 2000 Tea Plantation and Monson viewpoint.

Wat Phra That Doi Suthep

The most popular tourist attractions in the city, Wat Doi Suthep's central shrine contains a much-revered seated Buddha, the gallery and the chedis which represented Chiang Mai and Sukhothai styles. Travelers can reach the temple by hiking up the mountain, renting a motorbike, or hopping into a songthaew(red trucks). Tip: Visiting just before sunset is a special treat.

Mae Ya Waterfall

Waterfalls are considered as the best camping spot amidst a green forest park. There has s 30-storied cliff waterfall is a great place for getting soaked and making your mind and body fresh and has spot of the cliff to get a close view of the water sprinkle.

Central of Thailand

Capital City : Bangkok

Krung Thep Maha Nakhon (Bangkok), occupying most of the Chao Phraya basin, is the political, economic, and cultural core of old Siam and present-day Thailand. Commercial and industrial activity is heavily concentrated in the region, especially in Bangkok, and economic growth has been faster there than elsewhere.



ICONSIAM

There are over 7,000 shops in this 10-story mega lifestyle complex, perched across the renowned Chao Phraya River. Besides shopping and dining, art lovers can also appreciate the art-in-mall journey, with stellar works by over 100 Thai and international artists.

Wat Arun

One of the most iconic and beautiful temples in Thailand. It is the best place to overlook the Chao Phraya River and enjoy the night view. Its stunning towering spire is one of the landmarks of Bangkok.

Bangkok Art and Culture Centre

A major art centre, It is a place for people to meet, and to help define and make culture. It locates at the chosen area of the Pathumwan Junction. Tourists can participate in art and cultural activities, to learn and express themselves, at the same time becoming part of their recreational pursuits.

Yaowarat Road

Every five steps unveil a new food spot, and every single one of them offers amazing different type of foods and hearty spins on the local cuisine. The best part? You can walk through 24 hours night market road.

South of Thailand

'Krabi'

Krabi is the top three provience of South, charming city of the Andaman Sea. There has more than 130 large and small islands, with white sandy and sparkling water, abundant coral reefs including mangrove forests and caves.



Phi Phi Island

The island is the most famous for tourist destination, a peak of scenery to spectacular provided with accommodations, restaurants and facilities. There have a diving and snorkeling spots which is a paradise for divers.

Railay

Railay Beach has a limestone cliff. There has a viewpoint that is a very beautiful ridge. The highlight is the rock climbing activity.

Thale Waek

Location of beach is combined by three main islands, Koh Mor, Koh Tub and Koh Kai. Tide low is highlight as it will part white sand and water. When the tide is high, it is the best spot for swimming with a different kind of fishes.

Krabi Night Market

Night market is open every Friday to Sunday in evening. There are lots of food local food stalls for travelers.
